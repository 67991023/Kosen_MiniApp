5-Year Curriculum Overview

The KOSEN-KMITL curriculum is structured as a comprehensive 5-year program, divided into foundational, intermediate, and advanced stages. Each year builds upon the previous, ensuring progressive skill development and deep technical expertise.

Year 1: Foundation

Core Subjects

  • Mathematics I: Calculus and Linear Algebra
  • Physics I: Mechanics and Thermodynamics
  • Chemistry: General Chemistry and Laboratory Techniques
  • Engineering Drawing: Technical Drawing and CAD Basics
  • Computer Programming: Introduction to Programming (Python/C)
  • English for Engineers: Technical Communication Skills

Note: First-year students are required to maintain a GPA of at least 2.50 to continue in the program.

Year 2: Core Engineering Principles

Core Subjects

  • Mathematics II: Differential Equations and Complex Analysis
  • Physics II: Electricity and Magnetism
  • Materials Science: Properties and Applications of Engineering Materials
  • Circuit Analysis: DC and AC Circuits
  • Statics and Dynamics: Fundamental Mechanics
  • Data Structures: Advanced Programming Concepts

Students begin specialization track selection at the end of Year 2.

Year 3: Specialization Begins

Specialization Tracks

Students choose one of five engineering disciplines and begin focused coursework:

Mechanical Engineering Track:

  • Thermodynamics and Heat Transfer
  • Fluid Mechanics
  • Machine Design
  • Manufacturing Processes

Electrical Engineering Track:

  • Electronics and Circuits
  • Digital Systems Design
  • Signals and Systems
  • Power Systems Fundamentals

Computer Engineering Track:

  • Computer Architecture
  • Operating Systems
  • Database Systems
  • Software Engineering

Important: All students must complete a minimum of 3 laboratory courses per semester.

Year 4: Advanced Studies

Advanced Coursework

  • Advanced Topics in Specialization: Field-specific advanced courses
  • Engineering Project I: Team-based project design and implementation
  • Industrial Automation: PLC, SCADA, and control systems
  • Engineering Economics: Cost analysis and project management
  • Technical Japanese: Language preparation for international collaboration

Internship Opportunity

Year 4 students participate in a 3-month industry internship during summer break, gaining real-world experience with partner companies in Thailand and Japan.

Year 5: Capstone and Research

Final Year Requirements

  • Capstone Project: Year-long comprehensive engineering project
  • Research Methodology: Scientific research and documentation
  • Advanced Electives: Specialized topics in chosen field
  • Engineering Ethics: Professional responsibility and standards
  • Entrepreneurship: Innovation and business development

Graduation Requirements

  • Complete all required courses with minimum GPA of 2.50
  • Successfully complete and defend capstone project
  • Pass comprehensive examination
  • Complete minimum 400 hours of laboratory work
  • Demonstrate English proficiency (TOEIC 450+)

Additional Requirements

Mandatory Activities

  • Attend at least 2 technical seminars per semester
  • Participate in annual engineering competition
  • Complete safety training certification
  • Join at least one student technical organization

Important Policies

Students must maintain continuous enrollment throughout the 5-year program. Any interruption requires formal approval from the academic committee.

Assessment Methods

Student performance is evaluated through multiple methods:

  • Written examinations (40%)
  • Laboratory reports and practical work (30%)
  • Projects and presentations (20%)
  • Class participation and homework (10%)
