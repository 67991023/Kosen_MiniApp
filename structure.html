Program Structure & Curriculum

The KOSEN-KMITL program offers a comprehensive 5-year curriculum designed to provide students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills in engineering and technology.

Year 1-2: Foundation Studies

During the first two years, students build a strong foundation in:

  • Mathematics and Physics
  • Basic Engineering Principles
  • Computer Programming
  • Technical Drawing
  • English Communication
Go to Top

Year 3-4: Specialized Studies

In the middle years, students focus on specialized technical subjects:

  • Advanced Engineering Mathematics
  • Electronics and Circuit Design
  • Mechanical Systems
  • Control Systems
  • Software Engineering
  • Project Management
Important: Students must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.5 to continue in the specialized studies phase.
Go to Top

Year 5: Capstone Project

The final year is dedicated to:

  • Industry Internship (6 months)
  • Capstone Project Development
  • Research Methodology
  • Professional Ethics
  • Career Preparation

Students work on real-world projects in collaboration with industry partners, applying all the knowledge and skills acquired throughout the program.
